VERSION 2

The UnLimbited Arm v2.1, also known as the Alfie Edition, is a fully parametric assistive arm design from Team UnLimbited. It is designed so the parts can be generated to match a recipient’s measurements, printed, assembled, and thermo-formed into a usable device.

WHAT YOU NEED

To size a Version 2 device correctly, you need three measurements.

A: Upper arm circumference
Measure around the middle of the affected upper arm.

B: Forearm length
Measure from the inside fold of the elbow to the wrist on the non-affected arm.

C: Hand length
Measure from the wrist to the tip of the longest finger on the non-affected hand.

IMPORTANT NOTES

A must be taken from the affected arm.
B and C are taken from the non-affected arm.
Minimum hand length is 135 mm.
Minimum bicep circumference is 110 mm.
Minimum cuff length is 65 mm.

HOW SIZING WORKS

Version 2 uses those real-world measurements to scale the printable arm parts.

The main settings are:

Left or Right: choose the arm the device is for
Hand Length: wrist joint to fingertip
Forearm Length: wrist joint to elbow crease
Bicep Circumference: circumference of the upper arm

There are also two technical settings:

Cuff Length: usually leave at the default unless you have a specific reason to change it
Pin Hole Diameter: the metric bolt size used for the tensioner pins, typically M3, M4, or M5

RECOMMENDED PRINT SETTINGS

Printer: any suitable FDM printer
Material: PLA
Layer height: 0.2 mm
Infill: 35% honeycomb
Rafts: none
Supports: yes, but only for specific parts
Perimeters: 3 shells
Top layers: 3
Bottom layers: 3

SUPPORT GUIDANCE

The Palm area needs support.
The Cuff needs support.
None of the other parts require support.
For the Palm, Team UnLimbited typically adds 2 to 3 rows of support in the middle of the palm area to help with bridging.
For the Cuff, use auto-generated support.
All parts are printed in PLA to allow for easier thermo-forming after printing.

TYPICAL WORKFLOW

1. Measure the recipient
2. Enter the measurements into the generator
3. Generate the required parts
4. Print the parts
5. Assemble the device
6. Thermo-form and fit as needed

ABOUT THE DESIGN

The UnLimbited Arm v2.1 is the successor to the earlier Isabella Edition and was named the Alfie Edition after its first recipient.

Key design aims:

Fully parametric sizing
Simplified workflow
Generation of all required parts from recipient measurements
Support for a wide range of recipient sizes, limited mainly by printer build volume
